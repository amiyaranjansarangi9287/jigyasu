## Key Findings

### Critical Platform Gap
1. **Age Tier Missing**: Platform serves ages 3-12 but nothing for 13-16
   - This excludes the exact demographic that needs exam preparation
   - Class 10 students are a critical audience in India
   - First-generation learners like Rahul need this most

2. **Curriculum Alignment**: Content not aligned with state board exams
   - No mathematical formulas or numerical problems
   - No practice questions in exam format
   - No coverage of Class 10 specific topics

3. **Scientific Terminology**: Heavy transliteration instead of translation
   - "Newton", "Electron", "Proton" not translated
   - No Odia context for scientific concepts
   - Makes learning harder for students with limited English

### What Worked
1. **Offline Functionality**: Excellent for rural internet constraints
2. **Visual Learning**: Better than textbook for understanding concepts
3. **Odia Interface**: Basic interface in Odia works well
4. **Download Feature**: Critical for school computer lab scenario

### Issues for 15-16 Year Olds
1. **Content Too Basic**: Rahul found everything too easy
2. **No Exam Preparation**: Missing practice questions, formulas, numerical problems
3. **Childish Design**: Feels designed for younger children
4. **No Advanced Content**: No calculus, advanced physics, chemistry
5. **Maker Space Irrelevant**: Doesn't serve exam-focused goals

### Recommendations
1. **Add "Exam Prep" Tier**: Create content for 13-16 year olds aligned with state board syllabi
2. **Curriculum Alignment**: Map content to Class 9-10 curriculum for each state
3. **Scientific Translation**: Translate or properly contextualize scientific terms in Odia
4. **Practice Problems**: Add numerical problems and exam-style questions
5. **Formula Sheets**: Include downloadable formula sheets
6. **Previous Year Papers**: Add access to previous year board exam papers
7. **Mature Design**: Create a more mature interface for older students
8. **Batch Download**: Allow downloading entire subject content for offline study

## Conclusion
Rahul's experience revealed a critical gap in Jigyasu's design: the platform serves ages 3-12 but has nothing for 13-16 year olds preparing for board exams. This is exactly the demographic that needs free, quality education the most in rural India. The offline functionality works excellently, which is crucial for Rahul's context, but the content is too basic and not aligned with exam requirements. 

**Urgent Recommendation**: Add an "Exam Prep" tier for Class 9-12 students aligned with state board curricula. This would serve the platform's mission of equitable education by reaching students who need it most during the critical exam preparation years.

Without this addition, Jigyasu is failing a significant portion of its stated target audience ("ages 2 to 80+") and missing an opportunity to make a real impact on educational equity in India.
